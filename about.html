Computational Neuroscience

Neural Representation Space Transformation by Modifying Connection in V2 Neurons.
The work is to understand how neural representation will be reshpaed by instensively training a set of stimuli. Data was recorded from early visual cortex during presenting a set of Cartesian and Non-Cartesian images. In the preprocessing stage, I did semi-automatic spike sorting to collected data, discovered receptive field and tuning property of each neuron. t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t SNE) and Principle Component Analysis (PCA) were used to discover neural representation in low dimension space. To analyze the connectivity property of neurons, I appliedd Noise Correlation (NC), Cross-correlaiton histogram analysis (CCH). We found noise correlation increased for pairs of neurons that is highly co-activated (5% among all possible pairs). Also we found decoding accuray is also increased which is correlated with increase in NC.

Hierarchical Subcortical Sub-regional Shape Network Analysis.
To understand how Alzheimer's disease affect subcortical brain shape network, we gathered structural MRI data from Alzheimer's disease patients (AD) and healthy subjects (HC). Connectivity was measured based on the surface area correlation between different subcortical regions. Each subject is treated as one sample, correlation matrix for each group is cpmputed seperately. Connectivity matrix was thresholded from correlation matrix, then global efficiency and local efficiency was measured within each group and compared difference between each group. found significant HC-vs-AD group differences in the average local efficiency and average global efficiency. In addition, the local nodal efficiencies between the right thalamus and all three of the right hippocampus, right amygdala, and left thalamus, as well as that between the left amygdala and left hip- pocampus, decreased significantly in AD. The major work has been published in Neuroscience, Hierarchical Subcortical Sub-Regional Shape Network Analysis in Alzheimer’s Disease, 2017.

Neural Network Models of Primary Visual Cortex.
We used Regularized Linear Regression model (Lasso & Ridge), Fully Connected Neural Network, CNN and reduced version of Capsule neural network to model V1 cortex neuron responses to natural and grating images. Here input is the 65*65 images, the output is 37 well centered neurons' firing rate. Approved sparsity property of Neuron responses, by adding L1 Norm which improved prediction correlation from 0.599 to 0.63. All data from Odelia Schwartz's public dataset

A Near-Infraed Light Based Wearable Breast Cancer Detector.
During junior year, My teamates and I designed a wearable breast tumor detector based on Near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS). 735nm and 850nm diodes were used as light source. The ratio of oxyhemoglobin to deoxyhemoglobin was computed according to transmitted light intensity. The entire system built on the DSP processor with CMOS camera.
