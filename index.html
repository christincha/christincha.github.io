About Me

I am a fifth year PhD student at NeuroAI Lab, University of Washihngton, Seattle, advised by Prof.Eli Shlizerman. Before I join the University of Washington, I worked with Prof.Tai Sing Lee at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

My primary research interest lies in understanding neuron activities and the neural mechanism for behavior generation. In terms of method, I analyze brain activities through dynamic system, deep learning models. Broadly, I am interested in Computational Neuroscience, Brain Computer Interface, interpretability and explainability of Deep Learning System, Representation Learning.
